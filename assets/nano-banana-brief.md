# Nano Banana Design Brief — GreenWatt Report Hero Image

## What We Need
A hero image for the GreenWatt landing page showing a single, premium-looking report with a slight peek inside. This sits in the left column of a two-column layout next to an opt-in form. It needs to look like something worth downloading for free.

## The Image
One report — clean, professional cover with 1-2 interior pages peeking out behind or beside it, showing a glimpse of data tables, colored score indicators, or bar charts. The interior peek should be slightly angled or offset so you can see there's real substance inside, but not readable — just enough to create curiosity.

**Cover should show:**
- GreenWatt logo at top (text-based: "Green" in #16A34A + "Watt" in #1E293B, bold)
- Title: "Lead Quality Benchmark Report"
- Subtitle: "What Actually Matters — And What Doesn't"
- Bottom line: "GreenWatt Consulting"
- Clean white background with green accent bar at top

**Interior peek should show (partially visible, slightly blurred or at an angle):**
- A data table with rows and colored indicators (green, yellow, red dots or bars)
- Something that looks like a scorecard with pillar names and bar charts
- Enough structure to communicate "this has real data in it" without being readable

**What it should NOT look like:**
- Not a fan of 13 reports — just one
- Not a flat screenshot — needs depth/dimension (slight 3D angle, drop shadow)
- Not overly designed or flashy — clean consulting aesthetic

## Dimensions
- **Desktop**: Image sits in a column roughly 500-600px wide, next to a form
- **Mobile**: Full-width, stacks above the form
- Deliver as transparent PNG (no background) or on white

## Brand Guidelines

- Font: Inter (Google Fonts)
- Weights: 400, 600, 700, 800

### Logo
- Text-based: "Green" in #16A34A + "Watt" in #1E293B

- No icon — just the text

### Design Feel
- Clean, professional, data-driven
- Like a premium consulting firm's output
- Generous whitespace, sharp typography, subtle shadows
- Think: McKinsey meets modern SaaS
